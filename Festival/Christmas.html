Christmas

Christmas is celebrated on a large scale in Sri Lanka every December, even though the majority of citizens are Buddhists. The rule of European colonies has instilled the customs and traditions of this joyful period within Sri Lankan culture. Relatives and friends are invited to homes and lavish spreads are prepared along with the exchanging of gifts, fun and laughter. The streets, malls, hotels, restaurants and houses of Colombo are decorated with bright Christmas lights and Christmas trees are sold along the streets, filling the air with a festive aroma. Most churches hold midnight mass and carol service to celebrate the birth of Christ. Fireworks erupt, families gather to decorate the Christmas tree and happy carol singers travel from door to door. Most families, churches and charitable oraganisations help to spread Christmas cheer by donating gifts and food and organising Christmas celebrations for the less fortunate. Hotels & restaurants hold Christmas Eva and Christmas day specials, offering a feast of traditional Christmas favourites such as turkey, stuffing, eggnog and Christmas pudding.
